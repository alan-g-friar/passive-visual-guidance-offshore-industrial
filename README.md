# Beyond Emergency Lighting: Passive Visual Guidance Systems in Offshore, Marine, Energy and Industrial Facilities

A technical discussion paper exploring the potential role of passive visual
guidance systems as a supplementary safety layer in offshore, marine, energy,
and industrial facilities, examined through the lens of degraded visibility
conditions.

**Author:** Alan G. Friar, B.Sc. Electrical Engineering  
**ORCID:** [0009-0000-6986-1771](https://orcid.org/0009-0000-6986-1771)  
**Organisation:** EnBra Group LLC  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** July 2026

## Abstract

Modern offshore, marine, energy, and industrial facilities employ multiple,
well-engineered layers of protection intended to support personnel safety:  

- Primary lighting  
- Emergency lighting
- Battery-backed power
- Emergency generation
- Signage, and
- Established emergency response procedures.

Despite these protections, practical visibility challenges persist:  

- Structural obstructions
- Equipment density
- Severe weather
- Smoke
- Steam
- Power-related events, and
- Human factors

Can all degrade an individual's ability to identify escape routes,
locate emergency equipment, and recognise critical assets during abnormal conditions.

This paper examines whether passive visual guidance systems - photoluminescent
materials, reflective surfaces, and contrasting delineation - may offer a
valuable supplementary layer of visual and cognitive support, drawing on
established practice from aviation, maritime, and mining.  

Scope extends beyond offshore platforms to marine terminals, dockyards, and shipyard/dry
dock facilities. The paper does not propose new standards, advocate for
specific products, or replace existing lighting philosophies; it aims to
encourage professional discussion on whether further evaluation is warranted.
